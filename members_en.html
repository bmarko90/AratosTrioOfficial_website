MEMBERS



Katarina Popović received a master’s degree after graduating from the Faculty of Music, University of Arts in Belgrade, in the violin class of Prof. Marija Špengler. After postgraduate studies in chamber music at the same school, she enrolled artistic doctorate studies in the chamber music class of Prof. Goran Marinković. During doctoral studies she was engaged as a teaching fellow on the chamber music department under supervision of Prof. Ladislav Mezei. She attended various master classes with artists and pedagogues such as David Takeno, Gordan Nikolić, Stefan Milenković, Rafael Oleg, Orest Shourgot, Mira Glodeanu, Ilia Korol, David Drabek, Elisabeth Blumenstock, Jeremy Joseph, Frederick Haas, Ruben Dubrovsky, Daniele Caminiti and Martina Warecka. Katarina performs actively with three ensembles that realized a number of concerts in Serbia and abroad. She is a member of Belgrade Baroque ensemble devoted to historically-informed performance practice of 17th and 18th century music, playing period instruments, and as such, is one of the first ensembles of its kind in Serbia. With ensemble Musikkens retning: nord<->sør she performs thematic concerts in Norway and Serbia that are designed to enrich cultural connections between two countries. In 2014, with Mihailo Samoran and Vanja Šćepanović, she founded Aratos Trio, an ensemble devoted to performance of contemporary pieces written for clarinet, violin and piano. Katarina made concert appearances on festivals such as Belgrade music festival (BEMUS), Belgrade Summer festival (BELEF), Belgrade international review of composers, Belgrade organ festival, Belgrade harpsichord festival, Belgrade early music festival, Baroque music days Novi Sad, Salzkammergut Festwochen (Austria), Timisoara Early Music Festival (Romania), Carniarmonie (Italy), Nei suoni dei luochi (Italy). For eight years Katarina Popović was employed as a violin teacher in the School for musically gifted children in Ćuprija, Serbia with noticed pedagogical results. Her students won a number of prizes on music competitions in Serbia and abroad. Currently, she has been appointed an assistant professor for chamber music at the Faculty of Music, University of Arts in Belgrade.


Mihailo Samoran graduated in 2007. from the Faculty of Music Art in Belgrade, and received in 2008. Master’s Degree, at the same faculty, where he studied with Professor Ante Grgin. Currently he is a student of Doctoral artistic studies – DMA, in clarinet performance, in the class of Professor Ante Grgin, and also Doctoral artistic studies of chamber music, in the class of Professor Goran Marinković, at the Faculty of Music Art in Belgrade. He attended master classes with Professors Jiri Hlavac, Michel Lethiec and Alexander Neubauer, and chamber music master class held by the Ensemble Wien – Berlin, consisting of solists of the Vienna and Berlin Philharmonic. Mihailo Samoran was finalist of the International competition Jeunesses Musicales in Bucharest (Romania) in 2002, and the International competition Jeunesses Musicales in Belgrade (Serbia), in 2012. He held numerous performances in renowned concert halls in Belgrade, and performed as a soloist with the Symphony Orchestra of the Faculty of Music Art in Belgrade, Artistic Ensemble of the Ministry of Defence Stanislav Binički, and the orchestra Camerata Serbica. As a chamber musician he regulary performs with ensemble Balkan Academy. From season 2006/2007. Mihailo Samoran is a permanent member of the Belgrade Philharmonic Orchestra.


Vanja Šćepanović graduated in 2010. in the class of russian Professor Vladimir Bochkariov and ends his studies with the greatest success. Thereafter, he received in 2013. Master's Degree of Music at the Faculty of Music Art in Belgrade (Serbia) where he studied piano with Aleksandar Serdar, and Ljiljana Vukelja. Later, he continued piano studies and finished Specialized academic studies, at the same faculty. Currently he is a student of Doctoral artistic studies – DMA, department chamber music, with Professor Goran Marinković. During the study he was the winner of many awards in the categories of solo piano and chamber music at the country and abroad. 2008. he was also a winner of the first prize in chamber music Pula - Croatia. He has performed in prominent venues in Montenegro, Serbia, Croatia, Slovenia, Italy and Canada. He is a member of the chamber trio Aratos Trio, with which actively performs significant pieces of the XX century for this chamber trio. As a promising young artist he is winner awards of the University of Montenegro, award of ZAMTES (Department of International and Cultural Cooperation), the Ministry of Culture and the Government of Montenegro. Vanja has numerous piano master classes, of which is specifically allocates three-year piano master classes with the russian pianist Vladimir Ovchinnikov. He was also an active participant in the work of Master classes piano with eminent professors and pianists: Wei Yi Yang (USA), Alberto Portugese (England), Vladimir Krpan (Croatia), Kyioko Hashimoto (Japan), Ludvig Ginter (Austria), Rita Kinka (Serbia). Vanja is very early began to engage in educational work. His students are winners of numerous awards and were laureates in the country and abroad (particularly distinguished international competitions in Serbia, Montenegro, Macedonia, Italy and France). He was also a jury member for the category - piano solo, at the national and international competitions in Serbia and Montenegro.
